HOW TO USE THE SCROLLBAR

One can use the arrow buttons to scroll the page up or down. The plus buttons scrolls to the top of the next page, and the minus buttons scrolls to the top of the previous page. The elevator shows which page is currently being viewed. Clicking anywhere in the elevator region takes you to that point in the document.

ABOUT

Introduced in 1981, the Xerox Star was the first workstation that tried to incorporate some of the technologies that are now standard in modern personal computers such as icons, two button mouse, and ethernet networking. The Xerox Star was one of the first systems to have a scrollbar on the right hand side of the window, and to also have a horizontal scrollbar at the bottom of the window. The Star was developed at Xerox's Systems Development Department (SDD) in El Segundo, California, which had been established in 1977 under the direction of Don Massaro. A section of SDD, SDD North, was located in Palo Alto, California, and included some people borrowed from PARC. SDD's mission was to design the "Office of the future", a new system that would incorporate the best features of the Alto, was easy to use, and could automate many office tasks.
