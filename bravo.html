    SCROLLBARS
Xerox Alto - Bravo
1974
CREATOR
Xerox PARC
HOW TO USE THE SCROLLBAR

To operate the Bravo scroll the user moves the cursor over the left margin, and the cursor changes shape to a double arrow to indicate scrolling is possible. Using the mouse, clicking the left button will move the page down, the right button will scroll up, and the middle (mouse wheel) button will take the relative vertical mouse position and jump to that relative position in the document. For example, if you clicked the middle button at the top of the left margin, the document will scroll to the very top, and if you click the middle button halfway down the left margin, it will scroll the middle of the document.

ABOUT

Bravo was the first WYSIWYG (what you see is what you get) editor on the Xerox Alto personal computer. It was a modal editor—characters typed on the keyboard were usually commands to Bravo, except when in "insert" or "append" mode, in which case they were entered into the character buffer. Bravo made extensive use of the mouse for marking locations in the text, as well as selecting areas of the text, but it was not used for command entry. (Being at the very dawn of graphical user interface design, a non-command driven interface was judged too ambitious and possibly even inefficient and cumbersome.)
